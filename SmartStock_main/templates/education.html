Proper Food Storage Tips

Refrigerator (35°F-40°F)

Keep refrigerator at or below 40°F (4°C). Store raw meat, poultry and seafood on the bottom shelf to prevent dripping onto other foods.

Freezer (0°F or below)

Keep freezer at 0°F (-18°C) or below. Use freezer-safe containers or heavy-duty freezer bags and label with contents and date.

Pantry (50°F-70°F)

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Keep foods in airtight containers to prevent moisture and pest issues.

Food-Specific Storage Tips

Apples

Refrigerate, away from ethylene-sensitive produce

Tomatoes

Room temperature, away from direct sunlight

Bread

Cool, dry place in airtight container or freeze for longevity

Leafy Greens

Refrigerate in loose plastic bag with paper towel

Visual Assessment Guide: Is It Still Good?

Many foods are safe to eat even after their date labels have passed. Use these sensory indicators to determine if food is still good:

Dairy Products

Safe: Mild smell, normal color, no unusual texture

Spoiled: Sour smell, discoloration, lumpy texture, mold

Fruits & Vegetables

Safe: Minor blemishes, slight softening

Spoiled: Mold, sliminess, foul odor, severe discoloration

Meat & Poultry

Safe: Normal color, no strong odor

Spoiled: Slimy texture, foul smell, gray/green discoloration

Canned Goods

Safe: Intact can, normal appearance upon opening

Spoiled: Bulging cans, rust, spurting liquid when opened, mold

Food Preservation Techniques

Freezing

Almost any food can be frozen to extend shelf life. Proper wrapping prevents freezer burn. Label with date and contents.

Best for: Meat, bread, berries, prepared meals

Pickling

Preserves food in an acidic solution (vinegar) or brine to prevent bacterial growth.

Best for: Cucumbers, carrots, onions, peppers

Dehydrating

Removes moisture to prevent bacterial growth. Can be done with a dehydrator, oven, or sun-drying.

Best for: Fruits, herbs, vegetables, meat jerky

Food Recovery Hierarchy

The EPA's Food Recovery Hierarchy prioritizes actions to prevent and divert food waste:

1. Source Reduction: Reduce the volume of surplus food generated
2. Feed Hungry People: Donate extra food to food banks, soup kitchens, and shelters
3. Feed Animals: Divert food scraps to animal feed
4. Industrial Uses: Provide waste oils for rendering and fuel conversion
5. Composting: Create nutrient-rich soil amendment
6. Landfill/Incineration: Last resort disposal
